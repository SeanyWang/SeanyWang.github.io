Sean H. Wang

I am a Project Manager at the Max Planck Institute for the History of Science, where I coordinate the digital infrastructure collaborations in Department III: "Artefacts, Action, and Knowledge." I also co-edit ACME: An International Journal for Critical Geographies.

In addition, I am a PhD Candidate in Geography and Women's & Gender Studies at the Maxwell School of Syracuse University. My advisor is urban and cultural geographer Jamie Winders, who is an expert on immigration politics in the United States.

My dissertation is a comparative political ethnography of Chinese birth tourism in Hong Kong and southern California, where differing geopolitical contexts, immigration histories, and regional racial formations produced divergent local responses to the phenomenon.

I studied for an MA in Geography (2013) at the same institution, where I was supervised by Marxist cultural geographer Don Mitchell (now at Uppsala University in Sweden). I hold a BA in Geography (2011) from the University of Washington as well.
