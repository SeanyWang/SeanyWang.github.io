Sean H. Wang

A broadly trained human geographer, my primary research interests include citizenship, family, sexuality, and ethnic studies. I am experienced in various qualitative and quantitative research methods, and I have a strong interest in mixed-method research design. Besides work related to my dissertation research, I have published on topics such as gayborhoods in Seattle, queer foster and adoptive parents in New York state, and gender studies in South Africa. I am also involved in many collaborations, including projects on legal geographies of US citizenship in the Asia-Pacific with Emily Mitchell-Eaton, China's socioeconomic transformations and southern geographical theory with He Wang, and public transit equity with researchers affiliated with the eScience Instituteat the University of Washington.
